History

The Van Gogh museum first opened in 1973 with two buildings containing major works of Vincent from all his main periods. The famous Dutch architect Gerrit Rietveld, an important member of the architectual school 'De Stijl' was responsible for the design of the main building. 26 years later, in 1999, the museum opened a new building, designed by Kurokawa Kisho, who became famous as the designer of the Kuala Lumpur airport terminal. Visitors to the new building are welcomed by a beautiful, light, open design space. The Van Gogh museum is renowned worldwide and one of the main Amsterdam tourist destinations with over 2 million visitors per year. We would like to invite you and experience Van Gogh's work personally. You will find it is a fantastic experience.
